What is Imposter Syndrome?

Imposter syndrome is the persistent belief that your success is undeserved — that you've fooled people into thinking you're more competent than you really are, and sooner or later you'll be "found out."

It affects high achievers across all fields, and is especially common during career transitions, promotions, or when stepping into new roles.

This assessment is based on the Clance Impostor Phenomenon Scale (CIPS), a scientifically validated measure. It is not a diagnostic tool. If imposter feelings are significantly impacting your life, consider speaking with a professional.
